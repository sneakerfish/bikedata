Discover the Key Drivers of Bike-share Ridership in the Bay, Boston, and NYC Metropolitan Areas

Introduction

There exists little or no visualizations that facilitates the side-by-side comparison of bike-share system ridership.

Using data from three of the largest bike-share networks, this visualization EMPOWERS users to answer question: "What drives bike-share ridership?"

Comparing Bike-shares

While each city offers similar membership plans, Boston's Blue Bikes is significantly more affordable at almost 40% cheaper than New York City's annual plan.

New York City's system is significantly more extensive with more than 2x the number of stations than the next closest system

Bike-share System Trends Across Time

In terms of cumulative round trips, New York City due to its population has a larger number of cumulative trips since 2017.

The number of stations increased in each city since 2017, although gradually.

Bay Area

The Bay Area's ridership grew unevenly until the beginning of 2020 where ridership boomed.

However, ridership plummeted due to COVID19 pandemic and has not fully recovered.

It can be inferred that this might be due to major employers such as Apple, Facebook, and Google are continuing to support work-from-home policies until at least January 2022.

Boston Metro

Boston's ridership experienced continued steady growth with a drop during the start of the COVID19 pandemic.

Unlike the Bay Area, the Boston area's ridership has recovered above pre-COVID19 levels.

NYC Metro

Ridership in the NYC Metro follows a similar pattern to Boston in terms of ridership growth.

Unlike the Bay Area, major Wall St employers are actively encouraging workers to come back to the office

Moreover, NYC's mayor ordered local government employees back to the office on a full-time schedule.

General Trends

Many of the steep declines in the number of trips appear to occur over holidays or on a weekend.

Leading us to conclude that many people use bike-share systems primarily for their work-commute rather than pleasure

General Trends

Despite colder winters, Boston metro riders typically ride longer than their New Yorker and Bay Area counterparts.

All metros experienced a period of sustained longer median rides during the first wave of COVID19.

Seasonality

Due to the Boston and NYC Metros continental climate, it is clear that cyclist take longer rides as the weather gets warmer.

In contrast, the Bay Area's mediterranean climate does not experience the same seasonality in ridership.
